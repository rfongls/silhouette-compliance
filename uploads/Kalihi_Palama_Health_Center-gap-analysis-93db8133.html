Scope & Methodology

Purpose

Silhouette LLC is conducting this Combined Gap Analysis on behalf of Kalihi Palama Health Center to assess readiness across applicable regulatory frameworks. The objective of this engagement is to provide a clear, evidence-based view of where documentation and controls stand today, and to deliver a prioritized roadmap that guides the organization toward full compliance.

Standards Evaluated

  • NIST Standards
    • NIST SP 800-53 Rev 5 - Security and privacy controls framework for federal information systems, applied broadly to healthcare IT environments.
    • NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0 (CSF) - Voluntary framework providing a policy framework of computer security guidance for organizations to assess and improve their ability to prevent, detect, and respond to cyber attacks.
    • NIST SP 800-66 Rev 2 - NIST implementation guidance for applying the HIPAA Security Rule, providing detailed mappings between HIPAA requirements and NIST SP 800-53 controls.
  • HIPAA Security & Privacy Rules - Federal rules governing the protection and handling of protected health information (PHI) by covered entities and their business associates.
  • HITECH Act - HITECH Act provisions strengthening HIPAA enforcement, expanding breach notification requirements, and establishing civil money penalty tiers.

Methodology

The analysis was performed by reviewing the submitted document against current authoritative control text retrieved directly from official regulatory sources (ecfr.gov, csrc.nist.gov, hhs.gov, and cms.gov). Each control was evaluated for one of three statuses:

Yes - Addressed
Full credit. The control requirement is clearly documented and substantively addressed in the reviewed artifact.
Partial - Incomplete
Half credit. Some elements of the control are present but the requirement is not fully satisfied.
No - Not Addressed
Zero credit. The control is absent or not substantively addressed in the reviewed documentation.

Each control is scored as Yes (full credit), Partial (half credit), or No (no credit). A percentage score is calculated per standard by dividing credits earned by controls reviewed. An overall composite score averages across all evaluated standards. Risk levels reflect regulatory severity and organizational impact.

Compliant (85-100) - All or nearly all required controls addressed with adequate documentation
Partially Compliant (50-84) - Core controls present but gaps exist in required or addressable safeguards
Non-Compliant (<50) - Significant required controls absent; material regulatory exposure
